entry # 1

It was 8 am or maybe later. Does the time really matter? Moving on, the uditorium wasn’t too bad. It wasn’t cold or too warm. But the desks were small, my notebook definitely didn’t fit on the desk. Professor - whatever his name is - is talking and drawing diagrams. And I suppose I should pay attention, I muse to myself.

But it’s just the beginning of the semester, I still have some time don’t I. And it doesn’t seem too bad, as long as I study afterward, I rationalize. I have been on my phone way too much, well Instagram. And no one is replying back. I guess I’ll take a break. I look around the auditorium and it’s packed.

How many students really plan on doing electrical engineering or computer engineering? Every morning since the first class, I familiarized myself with who was in front of me on the left side and the center. I recognized everyone - well their faces, despite the masks. And today was different, seeing as someone with a cap was sitting in the center, one of the front rows. I wondered how they looked. Well, I wouldn’t be able to see how they looked now. And they were actively taking notes, so they were studious for sure, unlike me.

It was time to take notes.

entry # 2

It’s the next day. And it’s time for physics. Physics - the bane of my existence. Physics one was a nightmare. Physics two is plain torture. It’s only a few weeks in, and I’m terribly lost. I think maybe I’m just not cut out to be an engineer. But then again, it’s not like I’m trying. But the thing is, I’ve tried when I’m with myself to learn and understand. And it just doesn’t make any sense. Even while doing the homework as well.

Let’s not even talk about the quizzes. Those were a nightmare, absolute nightmares. I see I’m a liar; and we are indeed talking about the quizzes. I didn’t think it was possible, but I managed to make it possible. I got a 2/10 on my first quiz. Absolutely wonderful.

My second quiz I managed to outdo myself, and get 1.5/10. It says on the syllabus that only the two lowest quizzes get dropped. And right now, I’m just thinking how I can do any worse. But I know myself, and I’ll most likely get a 0 on my next quiz. I’m definitely not fit to be an engineer. Maybe I should give up on my future already.

Ok, no. That’s terrible thinking. I can’t just give up already. It’s still September. First month in, and I’m thinking of giving up. Shame on me.

Ok, let’s try to focus on physics while crying on the inside. I turn off my phone and get ready to write down whatever Professor - whatever his name is number two - drew, when I catch sight of a cap.

I turn my head slightly.

And no fucking way.

I think it’s cap boy from circuit class. But I am not sure.

I’m a fucking idiot for sure. But this requires an investigation. Forget physics; it’s not as important as this.

Maybe I’ll regret saying that. I don’t know.

entry # 3

Okay, so it’s now a few days later. So I noticed him - I sound like such a fucking creep - before and after class for a few days. And I’m pretty sure he’s in both of my classes.

But a good investigation needs evidence. So I’ll prove this with evidence. I noticed one day he carried a tote bag. And the next day, he brought a backpack. I waited a few more days, and it’s the same backpack. I’m not sure how it makes sense, but he’s definitely the same boy, in both my physics class and circuits class. And I don’t know why, but now I just want to be friends with him.

It’s like how I wanted to be friends with A in junior year of highschool. I really wanted to be friends with her. And despite social anxiety, I did it and started talking to her. And now we’re really good friends.

The problem is my social anxiety. It’s a fucking mess, and loves to mess with me. I keep on telling myself I’ll ask after class if he’s also taking physics. But that doesn’t go well. I’m like okay this day for sure. And it doesn’t happen. And the next time, too. I stay a little bit longer to ask.

But I’m a coward, but we all know that.

entry # 4

Okay. It’s wednesday, October 6. I’m in physics. And my stomach is swarming with butterflies, moths, everything. I’m feeling very anxious. And I still want to be friends with him. But I’m a mess, not the pretty kind, which would make it more tolerable. But then again, the universe has never been in my favor.

Ok, I’ll ask before class. I tell this to myself and repeat it over and over again. And he sits down in the seat beside me, though there is space. But same point. I’m pushing myself to ask. And then Professor - well I'm not going to say the whole thing again, frankly it takes too much effort to write and I am most definitely lazy - starts speaking.

To tell you the truth, I’m not surprised. I’m a pity case.

--

Finally it’s break time. Break time is like my savior. I cannot go too long thinking of physics without going insane.

I really want to ask and ask. But social anxiety has me in a steel grip. Okay, you know what. Fuck it. Screw it. I’m also really glad we have to wear masks, so he can’t see me turn red. And it’s not pretty.

I’m doing it. I’m doing it. I turn to my side, lean a bit forward and tap my knuckles on the desk. And he’s turning around.

Here it goes. “Do you, um, um, um -”

Gods, I’m such an embarrassment. “- take -” what’s that class called “- uh, uh, uh -”

My face is burning red. Thank God we wear masks otherwise I would die right there.

“- circuits -” that’s the class! “- with Professor - -”

He nods his head. So the investigation proved right as embarrassing as it was.

“I thought you looked familiar.” is the last thing I say before break ends.

Well, at least I got it over with. I’m still embarrassed. And I’m really glad we wear masks. Ok, back to physics.

--

Class is over finally. Thank God.

We leave the room, and his backpack is unzipped. Is this another conversation starter? Yes it is, and I’m taking it. It’s better than before. I tap him and tell him. And then it’s awkward. Wonderful.

But then he asks me my name. And I tell him. Then, I ask him his name.

'O'

It’s a pretty name.

And we’re headed our different ways now. But that’s it.

entry # 6

So today is the day of the physics exam. I look at the exam, and the only thing I know is my name. I want to cry and give up. Thank God they gave us formula sheets. Otherwise, I don’t even know anymore.

Okay, so I think I know how to answer this question. I remember how to do it - vaguely. I write down the equations and put in the numbers. I use my calculator and oh my god I hate scientific calculators so much. Why the fuck can’t we use graphing calculators? Like I keep on erasing my answer, and forgetting it. And we can’t store answers as variables.

Grr. And I don’t know what I’m doing.

My answer is 4509 N. And the answer choices are 43N, 25N, 16N, 30N. Well 4509 is closest to 43 right? Let’s bubble that.

And I don’t know how to answer any of the questions.

Ok, ok. I’m okay. I’m okay. I’m not going to die. No, I am going to die. I’m 19 and I’m going to die.

You know what, you know what? I’m done. I’m so fucking done.

Fuck this shit. I put answer choices on my eraser, and roll it.

Okay, for number 1, it’s choice A.

For number 2, it’s choice C.

For number 3, it’s choice B. And I go on.

Okay I’m on the last question, question 10. Maybe I should try to answer this question. I look at it, and I am so lost. Screw it, I’m putting C. I never want to do this again.

I pick up my papers and hand it to the lady in the front and all but run out of the room, relieved it’s over.
